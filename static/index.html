Power-outage plan (generator)

During an outage your generator can't run every unit at once without overloading. Set this up once and the app handles it automatically: the zones you choose switch off, the important ones take turns so they stay conditioned, and when normal power returns every zone goes back exactly as it was and your daily programs resume immediately.
Step 1
Which zones matter most?
The critical zones that must stay heated/cooled — e.g. a server room, or pipes that could freeze. The app keeps some running and rotates so none stays off for long.
Non-critical zones that can go without during the outage — e.g. a lobby or storage room.
Step 2
How should the 'keep running' zones share the generator?
Half is the safe default — half your critical zones run while the other half rest, then they swap.
The others switch off and wait for their turn.
How often they take turns. 15 minutes is gentle on the equipment (never below 5).
Auto keeps each zone between the Heat and Cool values below, heating or cooling as needed (best year-round; needs auto-changeover support). Choose Cool or Heat to force one — e.g. Cool in summer.
The temperature to maintain during a zone's 'on' turn.
Step 3
How does the app know the generator is on?
The app watches for a message from your transfer switch or generator monitor on your MQTT broker.
Currently listening on facility/generator/status — on = generator, off = utility.
